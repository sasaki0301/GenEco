1. What is Independent Assortment?

The Law of Independent Assortment states that alleles of different genes are distributed independently into gametes during meiosis. This means the inheritance of one trait (like flower color) does not influence another (like seed shape).

For example, in pea plants:

  • R = Round seed (dominant), r = Wrinkled seed (recessive)
  • Y = Yellow seed (dominant), y = Green seed (recessive)

A plant with genotype RrYy can produce four possible gametes: RY, Ry, rY, ry.

Genes for seed shape and color assort independently during gamete formation.

Chapter 2 Summary

  • Mendel discovered that traits are inherited through discrete units called genes.
  • Law of Dominance: Dominant alleles mask recessive ones.
  • Law of Segregation: Alleles separate during gamete formation.
  • Law of Independent Assortment: Alleles for different traits assort independently, creating variation.
