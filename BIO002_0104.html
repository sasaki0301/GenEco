1. What is Irritability?

Irritability is the ability of organisms to detect and respond to stimuli such as light, temperature, chemicals, or touch. These responses help them stay safe, grow properly, and maintain balance in their environment.

Tropism is a directional growth response of a plant toward or away from a stimulus. When the growth is toward the stimulus, it is called a positive tropism; when away, it is a negative tropism.

  • Thigmotropism: Response to touch â€” tendrils wrap around supports.

Taxis is the directional movement of an animal toward (positive) or away (negative) from a stimulus. It is a behavioral response and usually faster than plant tropisms.

  • Aerotaxis: Movement toward or away from oxygen (e.g., bacteria moving toward oxygen-rich areas).
  • Phototaxis: Movement in response to light (e.g., moths flying toward lamps â€” positive phototaxis).
  • Geotaxis: Response to gravity (e.g., insects avoiding downward surfaces).
  • Thigmotaxis: Movement in response to touch or contact (e.g., cockroaches hiding in tight crevices).
  • Chemotaxis: Movement in response to chemicals (e.g., sperm swimming toward egg chemicals).
