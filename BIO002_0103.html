Nutrition and Gas Exchange

Every living organism needs energy to survive. This energy comes from nutrients and oxygen — substances that enter the body through nutrition and gas exchange.

1. Why Organisms Need Nutrition and Gas Exchange

Nutrition provides materials for growth and repair, while gas exchange supplies oxygen for respiration and removes waste gases like carbon dioxide. These two processes are closely linked to how organisms obtain and use energy for survival.

Oxygen enters organisms and is used to release energy from food.

2. Modes of Nutrition

Organisms have evolved different ways to obtain food and nutrients:

  • Autotrophic nutrition: Organisms like plants make their own food through photosynthesis.
  • Heterotrophic nutrition: Animals and fungi depend on other organisms for food.

Activity 1: Classify the Example

Is a mushroom autotrophic or heterotrophic?

Answer: Heterotrophic — it absorbs nutrients from decaying organic matter.

3. Gas Exchange in Plants and Animals

Gas exchange allows organisms to take in oxygen and release carbon dioxide. This process supports cellular respiration — the reaction that converts food energy into ATP.

Plants use CO₂ for photosynthesis and release O₂; animals do the opposite during respiration.

Activity 2: Understanding Opposite Processes

How do the processes of photosynthesis and respiration complement each other?

Photosynthesis uses carbon dioxide and releases oxygen, while respiration uses oxygen and releases carbon dioxide. This balance maintains Earth's atmosphere and energy flow between organisms.

4. Gas Exchange in Humans

In humans, gas exchange occurs in the lungs, specifically in tiny air sacs called alveoli. Oxygen diffuses into the blood while carbon dioxide moves out to be exhaled.

Gas exchange happens through diffusion across thin alveolar walls.

Activity 3: Apply Your Knowledge

Why does the alveoli structure make gas exchange more efficient?

  • Large surface area for gas diffusion.
  • Thin walls for rapid diffusion.
  • Surrounded by capillaries for quick gas transport.

6. Lesson Summary

  • Nutrition provides materials and energy; gas exchange supplies oxygen and removes waste gases.
  • Autotrophs make their own food, while heterotrophs depend on others for nutrients.
  • Photosynthesis and respiration form a balanced gas exchange cycle between plants and animals.
  • In humans, alveoli in the lungs enable efficient gas exchange by diffusion.
