Non-Mendelian Inheritance Overview

Not all traits follow Mendel’s simple dominant–recessive patterns. Some traits are inherited through more complex genetic interactions.

1. What Is Non-Mendelian Inheritance?

Mendel’s laws explain many patterns of inheritance, but they do not explain all traits. Non-Mendelian inheritance refers to inheritance patterns that do not follow the Law of Dominance, Segregation, or Independent Assortment exactly.

In these cases:

  • Alleles may both be expressed
  • Traits may blend or show intermediate forms
  • More than two alleles may affect one trait
  • Traits may be linked to sex chromosomes

2. Why Mendel’s Laws Are Sometimes Limited

Mendel studied pea plants with traits controlled by single genes and simple dominance. However, many real-life traits involve:

  • Multiple alleles (more than two forms of a gene)
  • Multiple genes affecting one trait
  • Genes interacting with each other
  • Genes located on sex chromosomes

Because of this complexity, inheritance patterns can look very different from Mendel’s classic ratios.

3. Common Types of Non-Mendelian Inheritance

Some major types you will study include:

  • Incomplete Dominance – neither allele is completely dominant
  • Co-dominance – both alleles are fully expressed
  • Multiple Alleles – more than two alleles exist for a gene
  • Polygenic Inheritance – multiple genes control one trait
  • Sex-Linked Inheritance – traits linked to sex chromosomes

Activity 1: Identify the Pattern

A child has blood type AB. One parent has type A, and the other has type B. Which inheritance pattern does this show?

Answer: Co-dominance — both A and B alleles are fully expressed.

Activity 2: Think Beyond Mendel

Human height is controlled by many genes and varies continuously. Does this follow Mendelian inheritance?

No. Height is an example of polygenic inheritance because many genes contribute to the trait, producing a wide range of phenotypes.

4. Importance of Studying Non-Mendelian Inheritance

Understanding non-Mendelian inheritance helps explain:

  • Human genetic disorders
  • Variation within populations
  • Why traits don’t always fit simple ratios
  • How complex traits evolve

Lesson Summary

  • Not all traits follow Mendel’s laws exactly.
  • Non-Mendelian inheritance includes patterns like incomplete dominance and co-dominance.
  • Many traits are influenced by multiple genes or multiple alleles.
  • These patterns explain real-world biological variation.
